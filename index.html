What is a Civic Hack Day?

The Adler Planetarium is hosting a Civic Hack Day to participate in the nation-wide event, The National Day of Civic Hacking. Taking place on the 1st and 2nd of June, 2013, in major cities throughout the United States, The National Day of Civic Hacking will bring together average citizens, software developers, and entrepreneurs from across the nation to develop solutions to the challenges faced by our local neighborhoods, cities, states and country. They will collaboratively create, build, and invent using publicly-released data, code and technology. The National Day of Civic Hacking will provide citizens an opportunity to do what is most quintessentially American: roll up our sleeves, get involved and work together to improve our society and the place we call home.

How are youth agents of change?

As young people are often those who are most acutely aware of the problems in their communities, but are rarely empowered by our society to make a change, the Adler Planetarium will be inviting Chicago youth to participate in the Civic Hack Day.

This unique, youth-focused Civic Hack Day will embed young people with well-articulated and compelling problems into teams. A team will be a collection of skilled professionals including, but not limited to, engineers, scientists, web developers and designers who will work with the youth and their adult mentors to create technology solutions to the issues they face in their communities.

The Adler will host at least 5 teams, we need your help to make this event as powerful as possible. If you would like to submit a team for the day you can do so here or if you would like to donate money or resources to the event you can do so here.

How do I Participate?

We need 5 teams each consisting of 6 or 7 people, to sign up for the 28-hour, Civic Hack Day event. The event will kick off at 11am on the 1st of June and will finish at 2pm the following day. Teams will be invited to spend the night at the Adler and will be provided with plenty of food and caffeine to keep the momentum going!

In the week prior to the Civic Hack Day, each team will be paired by the Adler Planetarium with a small group of youth participants and their mentors, so that on the morning of June 1st, everyone can hit the ground running.

Your Team

Your team should consist of 6 or 7 people, whom have a range of different skill sets. Each person may bring the expertise of a Hacker, Project Manager or Domain Exper to the event, but it is quite likely that these roles will blur and diversify as the 28 hour event progresses! Sign up a team here.

  • Domain Expert

    This person is a recognized and respected content expert with significant and authentic experience and/or membership in the context, environment, community, governance, industry or network where the problem resides.

  • Hacker

    This person is a designer, engineer, scientist, programmer, artist, technician, educator, tinkerer, carpenter, welder, tradesman, or any skills expert who can help the team create something new.

  • Project Manager

    This person will manage the project deliverables, coordinate team roles and responsibilities, communicate progress and expectations, and keep team members focused on task.

Youth Participants and Mentors

When the youth participants join the team on the Civic Hack Day they will most likely take Problem Owner or Journalist roles. Their mentors, who will have technical skills, will participate as Hackers as well as making sure that the youth are well integrated into the teams.

  • Problem Owner

    This person has a problem they need help solving and some vision for a solution. They are an engaged member of the community, personally impacted by the problem they bring and a direct stakeholder in the design and outcomes of the solution.

  • Journalist

    This person is a creative content producer who will report on team accomplishments using digital and social media, [micro]blogs, or websites to tell a story about the team, their problem, and their progress towards a solution.

  • Mentor

    This person has what it takes to guide a young person to make a meaningful contribution to the team. They have strong enough professional experience and technical ability to be a Hacker themselves, as well as strong command of the teaching pedagogy needed to structure and scaffold the experience for young people.
